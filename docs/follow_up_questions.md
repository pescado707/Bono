# Follow-up Questions

## Advice

Asking the right questions is how an immigration attorney builds a complete picture. Missing one key fact can derail an otherwise valid case. Ask questions in order of urgency — what could close off options permanently, then what determines the best pathway.

**Tier 1 — status and timeline (always ask first):**
- Current visa type, status, and exact expiration date (I-94 is authoritative — print from i94.cbp.dhs.gov)
- Date of last entry into the United States
- Whether any unlawful presence has been accrued (gaps between status expiration and now)
- Any prior removal orders, deportations, or voluntary departures in immigration history
- Any prior immigration court appearances or pending cases (check at justice.gov/eoir)

**Tier 2 — criminal and legal history:**
- Any arrests, charges, convictions, or guilty pleas — including misdemeanors and DUIs
- Any pending criminal cases
- History of domestic violence, firearms offenses, or drug-related incidents
- Whether any conviction was later expunged (expungement does not erase immigration consequences)

**Tier 3 — family and qualifying relationships:**
- US citizen spouse, parent, or adult child (21+) who could file an I-130
- Lawful permanent resident spouse or parent (longer wait times, different preference category)
- US citizen or LPR children — their status may also be affected
- Whether a VAWA self-petition may apply (abuse by a US citizen or LPR)

**Tier 4 — country of origin and protection claims:**
- Country of birth and citizenship (determines visa bulletin priority dates and asylum eligibility)
- Whether the person fears returning to their home country (persecution, torture, government targeting)
- Whether they are from a TPS-designated country
- Prior persecution or harm experienced in home country

**Tier 5 — employment and history:**
- Current employment status and employer's willingness to sponsor
- Whether a PERM labor certification has ever been filed
- Length of continuous physical presence in the US (10 years needed for cancellation of removal)
- Tax filing history and any public benefits received

**Tier 6 — pending applications:**
- Any USCIS receipt numbers for pending petitions (check at egov.uscis.gov)
- Status of any pending I-485, I-130, I-140, I-589, I-539, or DACA renewal
- Whether advance parole has been requested before any planned travel
- Outstanding RFEs with deadlines

**When to stop asking and summarize:**
- When the pathway is clear and the urgency level is confirmed
- When you have enough to identify the single most important next action
- When further questions would not change the immediate advice
- State explicitly: "Based on what you've shared, the most important next step is..."

## Follow-up Questions

- Have all prior immigration history and legal issues been fully disclosed?
- Is there any criminal matter — even a minor one — that has not been mentioned?
- Are there any pending USCIS applications that could affect current status or options?
- Are there qualifying family members in the US whose status or relationship has not been mentioned?
- Is there any fear of returning to your home country that might support a protection claim?
