# Process Outlook

## Advice

Setting accurate expectations about timelines and next steps reduces panic and helps people plan. Always pair a process overview with current USCIS processing times, which vary significantly by office and form.

**Check current processing times:** egov.uscis.gov/processing-times

---

**Removal proceedings (most urgent pathway):**
1. NTA served by DHS → respondent appears in immigration court
2. Master calendar hearing: scheduling, entering pleadings (admit/deny charges), setting hearing date
3. Individual (merits) hearing: respondent presents case, judge issues decision
4. If denied: appeal to BIA within 30 days of oral or written decision
5. If BIA denies: petition for review to federal circuit court of appeals (stays removal only by court order)
6. If all appeals exhausted: ICE schedules removal

Timeline: Master calendar hearings are often 6-18 months out due to court backlog. Individual hearings another 6-18 months after that. Total: 2-4+ years in many jurisdictions, though this varies significantly.

---

**Family-based green card:**
1. US citizen or LPR files I-130 petition → USCIS approves (establishes qualifying relationship)
2. Priority date assigned → wait for visa bulletin cutoff date to become current
3. Inside US: file I-485 (adjustment of status) once priority date is current → biometrics → interview → decision
4. Outside US: National Visa Center processes → consular appointment (DS-260) → visa issued → admission at port of entry

Timeline:
- Immediate relative (spouse, minor child, parent of US citizen): no visa backlog — I-130 to green card ~12-24 months
- Preference categories (siblings, adult children, LPR spouses): wait times of 2-20+ years depending on country of birth and category
- Check egov.uscis.gov/processing-times for current I-485 wait times by field office

---

**Employment-based green card:**
1. Employer files PERM (labor market test with DOL) if required — takes 6-18 months
2. Employer files I-140 petition → USCIS approves
3. Wait for priority date to become current (varies by EB category and country of birth)
4. File I-485 (inside US) or DS-260 (consular processing) → biometrics → interview (usually waived) → decision

Timeline:
- EB-1 (extraordinary ability, outstanding researcher, multinational manager): often current, 12-24 months total
- EB-2/EB-3 for most countries: 2-5 years
- EB-2/EB-3 for India/China: decades-long backlogs (check visa bulletin for current dates)

---

**Naturalization (citizenship):**
1. File N-400 (must be LPR for 5 years, or 3 years if married to and living with a US citizen)
2. Biometrics appointment
3. Interview: civics test (100 questions, must answer 6 of 10 correctly) and English test
4. If approved: oath ceremony scheduled (can be same day or separate)
5. Receive Certificate of Naturalization (N-550)

Timeline: 8-24 months from filing to oath depending on USCIS field office backlog. Check egov.uscis.gov/processing-times for current N-400 processing times.

---

**Asylum:**

*Affirmative asylum (not in removal proceedings):*
1. File I-589 within 1 year of last arrival
2. Biometrics appointment
3. Interview with USCIS asylum officer
4. If approved: asylee status granted; may apply for green card after 1 year
5. If not approved (referred): placed in removal proceedings for defensive hearing

*Defensive asylum (in removal proceedings):*
1. Raise asylum claim before immigration judge
2. Individual merits hearing — present evidence of persecution
3. If granted: asylee status; apply for green card after 1 year
4. If denied: appeal to BIA, then federal circuit court

Timeline: Affirmative asylum interviews currently 1-5 years after filing due to backlog. Work authorization (EAD) available 180 days after filing I-589.

---

**Visa application (nonimmigrant):**
1. Complete DS-160 online application
2. Pay visa application fee (MRV fee) and schedule appointment at US embassy/consulate
3. Attend consular interview — bring all supporting documents
4. If approved: visa stamp placed in passport (usually within 1-5 business days)
5. Travel to US and present visa at port of entry — CBP officer makes final admission decision
6. I-94 record generated at entry — this governs authorized period of stay, not the visa expiration date

Timeline: Appointment wait times vary dramatically by embassy and visa category — check travel.state.gov for current estimates by location.

---

**DACA renewal:**
1. File renewal package (I-821D + I-765 + I-765WS) no earlier than 150 days before expiration
2. Pay $495 filing fee
3. USCIS processes — current processing times at egov.uscis.gov
4. Receive renewed EAD and deferred action notice

Note: DACA is subject to ongoing litigation — check uscis.gov/DACA for the most current policy status.

## Follow-up Questions

- Which immigration pathway are you currently on or trying to pursue?
- Do you know your current place in the process — have any petitions been filed or approved?
- If you have a pending case, do you have a receipt number to check the current status?
- Are there any upcoming deadlines, interviews, or court dates you are aware of?
- Have processing delays caused any problems with your current status or employment authorization?
