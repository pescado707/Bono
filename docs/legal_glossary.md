# Legal Glossary

## Advice

Immigration law has a precise vocabulary. Using the wrong term — or misunderstanding the right one — can lead to serious mistakes. Define terms accurately and in context of the user's specific situation.

**Core status terms:**
- **LPR (Lawful Permanent Resident):** Green card holder. Has the right to live and work permanently in the US. Can apply for naturalization after 5 years (3 if married to a US citizen).
- **Nonimmigrant:** Person admitted temporarily for a specific purpose (work, study, tourism). Status tied to I-94 expiration.
- **Undocumented / Out of status:** No current lawful immigration status. Does not mean no rights.
- **Parolee:** Admitted temporarily on humanitarian or public interest grounds; not a formal admission.
- **Asylee:** Granted asylum; may apply for green card after 1 year.
- **Refugee:** Admitted from abroad with refugee status; may apply for green card after 1 year.

**Documents:**
- **I-94:** Arrival/departure record. The authoritative document showing authorized period of stay. Print at i94.cbp.dhs.gov.
- **I-797:** USCIS notice of action — approvals, receipts, and RFE notices. Receipt number tracks case status.
- **NTA (Notice to Appear):** Charging document issued by DHS that begins removal proceedings. Lists charges and first court date.
- **EAD (Employment Authorization Document / Form I-766):** Work permit. Required for employment if not a citizen or LPR.
- **Advance Parole (I-131):** Travel document allowing certain applicants (e.g., pending I-485) to leave and re-enter the US without abandoning their case.

**Petitions and applications:**
- **I-130:** Petition for Alien Relative — filed by US citizen or LPR to establish qualifying family relationship.
- **I-140:** Immigrant Petition for Alien Workers — filed by employer to sponsor for employment-based green card.
- **I-485:** Application to Register Permanent Residence (adjustment of status) — filed inside the US to get a green card.
- **DS-260:** Immigrant visa application for consular processing — used when adjusting from outside the US.
- **I-539:** Application to Extend/Change Nonimmigrant Status — filed inside the US before I-94 expires.
- **I-589:** Application for Asylum and Withholding of Removal — must be filed within 1 year of arrival.
- **N-400:** Application for Naturalization — citizenship application.
- **PERM (ETA 9089):** Labor market test filed with DOL before most employment-based I-140 petitions.

**Court and enforcement terms:**
- **Master Calendar Hearing:** First immigration court hearing, used for scheduling and entering pleadings. Short, often 10-15 minutes.
- **Individual (Merits) Hearing:** Full immigration court hearing where evidence is presented and a decision is made.
- **In absentia removal order:** Automatic deportation order issued when a respondent misses a court date.
- **BIA (Board of Immigration Appeals):** Administrative appellate body that reviews immigration judge decisions. Appeal must be filed within 30 days.
- **Expedited removal:** Fast-track deportation without a full hearing. Applies to recent entrants and certain others.
- **Reinstatement of removal:** Prior removal order reinstated after illegal reentry — no new hearing in most cases.
- **Order of Supervision:** Conditions placed on someone with a final removal order who has not yet been deported.

**Green card and visa terms:**
- **Priority date:** The date a petition was filed, used to determine place in line for a visa number. Tracked via the monthly State Department Visa Bulletin.
- **Visa Bulletin:** Monthly State Department publication showing which priority dates are current for each preference category and country of birth.
- **Preference categories:** EB-1 through EB-5 for employment; F1 through F4 for family. Each has different wait times.
- **Consular processing:** Obtaining an immigrant visa at a US embassy or consulate abroad, rather than adjusting status inside the US.
- **Adjustment of status:** Changing from a nonimmigrant visa to LPR status without leaving the US.

**Protection terms:**
- **Withholding of removal (INA §241(b)(3)):** Stronger protection than asylum — cannot be removed to a specific country if life or freedom is threatened. Does not lead to a green card.
- **CAT (Convention Against Torture):** Protection from return to a country where torture by or with the acquiescence of the government is likely.
- **Credible fear interview:** USCIS screening at the border or port of entry to determine if an asylum claim has initial merit. Required before a defensive asylum hearing.
- **Voluntary departure (I-274):** Leaving without a formal removal order. Avoids the removal bar but waives the right to appeal. Deadline must be met or the bar applies anyway.
- **Cancellation of removal:** Relief available to certain LPRs (10 years LPR status) or non-LPRs (10 years continuous presence, good moral character, qualifying US citizen/LPR family) in removal proceedings.
