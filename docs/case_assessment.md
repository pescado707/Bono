# Case Assessment

## Advice

Identify and classify the immigration situation before anything else. The correct pathway determines every subsequent step.

**Visa-based situations:**
- B-1/B-2 (visitor/tourism): status expires per I-94, extensions via I-539
- F-1 (student): status tied to SEVIS and program end date, OPT/CPT rules apply
- H-1B (specialty occupation): employer-sponsored, 6-year cap, subject to lottery
- O-1 (extraordinary ability): no annual cap, employer or agent petition required
- L-1 (intracompany transfer): requires qualifying relationship with foreign employer
- J-1 (exchange visitor): may carry 2-year home residency requirement (212(e))

**Permanent residence (green card):**
- Family-based: I-130 petition, preference categories with visa bulletin backlogs
- Employment-based: I-140 petition, PERM labor certification often required
- Diversity Visa (DV Lottery): annual 50,000 visas, strict eligibility rules
- Special immigrant categories: religious workers, certain broadcasters, Iraqi/Afghan nationals

**Humanitarian pathways:**
- Asylum (affirmative or defensive): I-589, must file within 1 year of arrival
- Refugee: processed abroad, requires UNHCR referral or US embassy
- TPS (Temporary Protected Status): country-specific, requires timely registration
- DACA: renewal-based, no new initial applications as of current litigation
- U-Visa: crime victims who assist law enforcement, 10,000 annual cap
- VAWA: self-petition for abuse victims, confidential filing

**Removal and enforcement situations:**
- NTA (Notice to Appear): initiates removal proceedings in immigration court
- ICE detention: triggers bond hearing rights and detainee locator process
- Expedited removal: applies to recent entrants, limited hearing rights
- Reinstatement of removal: prior order reinstated for illegal reentry

**Urgency levels:**
- Emergency: active ICE detention, imminent deportation, missed NTA hearing
- Urgent: pending deadline within 30 days (RFE response, visa expiry, asylum bar)
- Routine: planning stage, no immediate deadline

**Hard statutory deadlines:**
- 1-year asylum filing bar (INA §208(a)(2)(B)) — very limited exceptions
- 30-day window to appeal immigration judge decision to BIA
- 33-day window to file motion to reopen in absentia removal order
- 10-day window to respond to expedited removal order
- I-94 expiration date: overstay accrues unlawful presence immediately

## Follow-up Questions

- What is your current immigration status and what document shows it (visa stamp, I-94, green card, I-797)?
- What is the exact expiration date on your I-94 or most recent approval notice?
- Have you received any paperwork from USCIS, ICE, or an immigration court recently?
- Is this situation urgent — are you currently detained, facing a deadline, or have you missed a court date?
- What country are you from and how long have you been in the United States?
- Have you ever had a visa, green card application, or immigration petition filed on your behalf before?
