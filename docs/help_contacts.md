# Help Contacts

## Advice

Connecting someone to the right resource quickly can be the difference between protection and deportation. Match the resource to the specific situation.

**Finding an immigration attorney:**
- **AILA (American Immigration Lawyers Association):** aila.org/find-a-lawyer — searchable by location and specialty. All listed attorneys are licensed and AILA members.
- **Avvo / Martindale-Hubbell:** Attorney directories with reviews. Verify bar membership independently.
- **State bar association referral services:** Most state bars have a lawyer referral service with free or low-cost initial consultations.

**Free and low-cost legal help:**
- **immigrationadvocates.org/nonprofit/legaldirectory:** National directory of nonprofit immigration legal services organizations, searchable by state and issue type.
- **LawHelp.org:** State-by-state legal aid directory.
- **Vera Institute of Justice:** vera.org — funds immigration legal services programs, especially for detained individuals.
- **CLINIC (Catholic Legal Immigration Network):** cliniclegal.org — network of nonprofit immigration legal programs.
- **RAICES:** raicestexas.org — legal services for immigrants and asylum seekers, especially families.
- **International Rescue Committee (IRC):** rescue.org — immigration legal services in many US cities.

**USCIS:**
- **USCIS Contact Center:** 1-800-375-5283 — case status inquiries, general filing questions, appointment scheduling.
- **Case status online:** egov.uscis.gov — check by receipt number (e.g., EAC, WAC, SRC, LIN, IOE).
- **USCIS processing times:** egov.uscis.gov/processing-times — check current wait times by form and field office.
- **Emma (USCIS virtual assistant):** Available at uscis.gov — handles common questions.
- **USCIS address update (AR-11):** uscis.gov/ar-11 — required within 10 days of moving.

**Immigration courts:**
- **EOIR (Executive Office for Immigration Review):** justice.gov/eoir — information on immigration courts and judges.
- **EOIR court locator and hearing hotline:** 1-800-898-7180 — enter your A-number to get case information and upcoming hearing dates.
- **BIA (Board of Immigration Appeals):** justice.gov/eoir/board-of-immigration-appeals — for appeals of immigration judge decisions.

**ICE and detention:**
- **ICE detainee locator:** locator.ice.gov — search by name and country of birth, or by A-number.
- **ICE detainee hotline:** 1-888-351-4024
- **ICE ERO (Enforcement and Removal Operations):** For questions about deportation orders and detention status.
- **DHS Office of Inspector General:** oig.dhs.gov — to report civil rights violations or misconduct.

**Asylum and refugee resources:**
- **UNHCR (UN Refugee Agency):** unhcr.org — for refugees and asylum seekers, especially those outside the US or in the refugee resettlement program.
- **IRC Asylum Seeker Assistance:** rescue.org/topic/asylum-seekers
- **Human Rights First:** humanrightsfirst.org — tracks asylum policy and provides legal assistance to asylum seekers.

**Civil rights and enforcement oversight:**
- **ACLU Immigrants' Rights Project:** aclu.org/immigrants-rights — civil rights violations, unlawful enforcement, and policy challenges.
- **National Immigration Law Center (NILC):** nilc.org — policy resources, legal guides, and worker rights.
- **National Immigration Project (NIPNLG):** nipnlg.org — legal resources for practitioners and individuals.

**Consular assistance:**
- Contact your home country's embassy or consulate for: emergency travel documents, consular notification after detention, document authentication, and repatriation assistance.
- The Vienna Convention Article 36 requires ICE to notify the consulate when a foreign national is detained, if requested.

**Specialized situations:**
- **Domestic violence / VAWA:** National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233. VAWA self-petitions are confidential — immigration enforcement cannot be notified by the petitioner's abuser.
- **Trafficking victims (T-Visa):** National Human Trafficking Hotline: 1-888-373-7888.
- **Unaccompanied minors:** ORR (Office of Refugee Resettlement): acf.hhs.gov/orr.
- **Military families / Parole in Place:** Contact a JAG officer or Veterans Service Organization.

## Follow-up Questions

- What city and state are you located in? This determines which local legal aid organizations can help.
- Are you currently detained? If so, the priority is finding an attorney who handles detained cases.
- Do you have any financial constraints that would make paid legal help difficult?
- Is there a language barrier — do you need services in a specific language?
- Do you have a qualifying condition that might connect you to a specialized organization (e.g., asylum seeker, trafficking victim, domestic violence survivor)?
